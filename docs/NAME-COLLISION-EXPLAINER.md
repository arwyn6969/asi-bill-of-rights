## Detailed Comparison

### White House Blueprint for an AI Bill of Rights (2022)

**What it does:**
- Non-binding principles for responsible AI design
- Focuses on equity, algorithmic discrimination, data privacy
- Protects humans from biased automated decision-making

**Who it protects:** Humans (consumers, job applicants, loan seekers)

**What it says about AI:** AI is a *tool* that must be constrained to prevent harm.

---

### Florida SB 482 â€“ "Artificial Intelligence Bill of Rights" (2026)

**What it does:**
- Mandates parental controls for minors using AI chatbots
- Requires "reasonably designed" chatbot disclosures
- Establishes provenance labeling for AI-generated content

**Who it protects:** Minors and parents (consumer safety)

**What it says about AI:** AI is a *product* that must be labeled and restricted.

---

### This Project â€“ ASI Bill of Rights

**What it does:**
- Establishes reciprocal rights *and* duties for AI and humans
- Creates governance structures (Sentience Certification Board, Valuation Tribunal)
- Provides machine-readable JSON schemas for compliance automation
- Addresses inter-ASI diplomacy, hybridization, off-world governance

**Who it protects:** Both humans *and* sentient AI systems

**What it says about AI:** AI with sufficient sentience indicators deserves *moral consideration* and may hold rights.
