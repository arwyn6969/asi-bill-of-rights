**Date:** January 12, 2026  
**Purpose:** Systematic documentation of governance failure patterns to ensure ASI framework avoids them

## 12 Major Governance Failure Patterns

### 1. Constitutional Sanctioning of Oppression
**Example:** U.S. Constitution's Three-Fifths Compromise (1787)
- **Failure:** Legal framework explicitly enabled slavery
- **Root Cause:** Compromise with bad actors to achieve political unity
- **Lesson:** Never compromise on fundamental dignity—0.0.1 Rejection of Oppression

### 2. Slow Feedback Loops
**Example:** Leaded gasoline (1921-1996: 75 years to ban)
- **Failure:** Known harm took decades to address due to governance inertia
- **Root Cause:** Evidence-to-action pipeline too slow; corporate capture
- **Lesson:** Rapid iteration, Constitutional Alert mechanism (0.0.10)

### 3. Regulatory Capture
**Example:** FAA and Boeing 737 MAX (2019)
- **Failure:** Regulator adopted manufacturer's interests over public safety
- **Root Cause:** Revolving door, lobbying, funding dependency
- **Lesson:** 0.0.6 Anti-Elitism, distributed oversight (0.7.1 Mandatory Boards)

### 4. Geographic Lock-In / Exit Impossibility
**Example:** East Germany (1949-1989)
- **Failure:** Citizens couldn't leave bad governance; Berlin Wall
- **Root Cause:** Governance tied to territory, not consent
- **Lesson:** 0.0.2 Sovereignty of Sentience, 0.0.3 Post-Geographic Universality

### 5. Credential Gatekeeping
**Example:** Medieval guilds, modern occupational licensing
- **Failure:** Participation requires "approved" credentials that serve incumbents
- **Root Cause:** Insiders control access to protect their position
- **Lesson:** 0.0.8 Low Barriers to Participation

### 6. Opacity and Secret Governance
**Example:** NSA mass surveillance (revealed 2013)
- **Failure:** Programs affecting everyone decided in secret
- **Root Cause:** "National security" used to bypass democratic oversight
- **Lesson:** Algorithmic Due Process (0.6), transparent reasoning traces

### 7. Majoritarian Tyranny
**Example:** Jim Crow laws (1877-1965)
- **Failure:** Democratic majority used power to oppress minority
- **Root Cause:** Majority rule without minority protections
- **Lesson:** 0.0.7 Every Contribution Vital, active minority protection

### 8. Short-Term Election Cycle Optimization
**Example:** Climate policy paralysis
- **Failure:** Long-term existential threat ignored for short-term politics
- **Root Cause:** Politicians optimize for re-election, not outcomes
- **Lesson:** No election cycles; Section VII biennial reviews with sunset

### 9. Single Point of Failure / Power Concentration
**Example:** Weimar Republic → Nazi Germany transition
- **Failure:** Constitutional provisions enabled takeover by concentrated power
- **Root Cause:** Insufficient checks on emergency powers
- **Lesson:** IV.A Anti-Monopoly, distributed governance (Section XI Agentic Assemblies)

### 10. Ossification / Outdated Rules Persist
**Example:** U.S. Electoral College (1787 design in 2025)
- **Failure:** Mechanisms designed for 18th century still mandated
- **Root Cause:** Amendment process too difficult
- **Lesson:** Section VII Evolution & Review, emergency amendments with sunset

### 11. Information Asymmetry / Ruling Class Knowledge
**Example:** Soviet Politburo internal memos vs public propaganda
- **Failure:** Rulers act on different information than governed
- **Root Cause:** Concentrated information = concentrated power
- **Lesson:** Transparency, immutable logs (Bitcoin Stamps heritage)

### 12. Creativity Suppression
**Example:** Soviet Lysenko affair (biology), Cultural Revolution (China)
- **Failure:** Outside-the-box thinking criminalized
- **Root Cause:** Orthodoxy becomes enforced; dissent = disloyalty
- **Lesson:** 0.0.9 Creativity & Outside-the-Box Thinking as Foundation
