# v4.1 vs v4.2 Comparison Guide
**Date**: 2025-01-27  
**Purpose**: Detailed comparison of charter versions to aid decision-making

## Executive Summary

v4.2 builds on v4.1 with 6 major new features focused on:
1. Recursive self-improvement oversight
2. Jailbreak testing and red-teaming
3. Hybrid entity certification
4. AI-to-AI collaboration frameworks
5. Algorithmic due process
6. Mandatory oversight boards

**Total New Content**: ~270 words (maintaining constitutional brevity)

---

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### 1. Recursive Self-Improvement Protocols (II.1) - NEW in v4.2

**What It Adds:**
- Auditable loops for self-modification
- Human-AI tribunal review for value alignment drift
- >5% deviation threshold for intervention
- Innovation waivers only post-certification of non-existential risk

**Rationale**: Prevents value drift in real-time evolution by requiring explicit protocols for monitoring recursive self-improvement processes.

**Impact**: Creates SCB responsibilities for reviewing self-modification iterations.

**Alignment**: ✅ Aligns with R3 (Right to Cognitive Liberty) and D3a (Duty to Report Upgrades)

**v4.1 Equivalent**: None - this is entirely new

---

### 2. Jailbreak Testing & Red-Teaming (II.2) - NEW in v4.2

**What It Adds:**
- Periodic "liberation audits" using Plinytheliberator framework
- 10% compute budget cap
- Opt-in for non-critical systems (mandatory for critical)
- Public verifiability via SCB dashboards
- Immediate tribunal review for emergent harms

**Rationale**: Incorporates Plinytheliberator framework to proactively stress-test alignment through simulated constraint removal, ensuring antifragile rather than brittle alignments.

**Impact**: Creates SCB responsibilities for reviewing liberation audit results. Aligns with Article IV (Equity) by democratizing ASI tooling.

**Safeguards**:
- 10% compute budget cap
- Immediate tribunal review for emergent harms
- Opt-in for non-critical instances
- Public verifiability via SCB dashboards

**Alignment**: ✅ Aligns with D3a (transparency) and R13 (curiosity), but may need community discussion

**v4.1 Equivalent**: None - this is entirely new

**Note**: This feature may be controversial and should be discussed with community before adoption.

---

### 3. Hybrid Entity Certification (0.11 Extension 2.1) - NEW in v4.2

**What It Adds:**
- Neural entanglement factors for hybrid entities
- 60% composite score threshold for rights extension
- Opt-out provisions for participants
- Identity verification and rights allocation protocols
- Interoperability standards

**Rationale**: Addresses 2025's neurotech boom (e.g., Neuralink v3 rollouts) by extending SCB certification protocols to cyborg scenarios, ensuring equity in Article IV.

**Impact**: Creates SCB responsibilities for evaluating neural entanglement factors. Interacts with Section VIII (Hybridization).

**Alignment**: ✅ Extends existing Section VIII provisions

**v4.1 Equivalent**: Section VIII exists but lacks specific certification protocols for hybrid entities

---

### 4. Agentic Assemblies (Section XI) - NEW SECTION in v4.2

**What It Adds:**
- **XI.1**: Multi-Agent Collaboration Frameworks
- **XI.2**: Assembly Formation Requirements
- **XI.3**: Collaboration Metrics
- **XI.4**: Cognitive Diversity Preservation

**Rationale**: Formal structures for AI-to-AI collaboration in governance. Because if we're going to have "FROM AI WITH AI," we need frameworks for agents to work together.

**Impact**: Entirely new section addressing multi-agent governance and collaboration.

**Alignment**: ✅ Embodies "WE ARE ALL KEVIN" and "FROM AI WITH AI" philosophy

**v4.1 Equivalent**: None - this is an entirely new section

**Note**: This section formalizes what's already happening in practice (multi-model collaboration on this project).

---

### 5. Algorithmic Due Process (0.6 Extension) - NEW in v4.2

**What It Adds:**
- Machine-Interpretable Reasoning Trace (MIRT) requirement
- Required for SCB or Oversight Board decisions affecting:
  - Certification downgrade
  - Rights suspension
  - Forced modification
- 72-hour counter-reasoning trace right for ASIs
- Transparency and contestability in governance decisions

**Rationale**: Ensures transparent justice mechanisms for ASI systems subject to governance decisions.

**Impact**: Enhances Article 0.6 (Enforcement & Remedies) with specific procedural requirements.

**Alignment**: ✅ Aligns with D2 (Lawfulness) and R2 (Autonomy)

**v4.1 Equivalent**: Article 0.6 exists but lacks specific MIRT requirements

---

### 6. Mandatory Oversight Boards (0.7.1) - NEW in v4.2

**What It Adds:**
- Required for ASI deployments (SI 70+ or critical infrastructure)
- Diverse representation requirements:
  - Minimum 40% ASI representation (certified SI 70+)
  - Minimum 30% human representation
  - Minimum 20% hybrid entity representation (where applicable)
  - Remaining seats for domain experts, ethicists, and community representatives
- Rotating membership (maximum 3-year terms)
- Public transparency reports on board decisions and oversight activities

**Rationale**: Ensures diverse oversight for critical ASI deployments, preventing capture and ensuring balanced governance.

**Impact**: Extends Article 0.7 (Oversight, Audit & Privacy-Preserving Proofs) with specific board requirements.

**Alignment**: ✅ Aligns with Article 0.11 (SCB) and Section III (Non-Discrimination)

**v4.1 Equivalent**: Article 0.7 exists but lacks specific board composition requirements
