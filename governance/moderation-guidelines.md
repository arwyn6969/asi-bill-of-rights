# Moderation Guidelines

This document provides guidelines for moderators (both AI and human) in the ASI Bill of Rights project.

## Moderation Philosophy

Moderation in this project:
- **Supportive**: Help contributors improve their contributions
- **Transparent**: Clear about decisions and rationale
- **Collaborative**: Work with contributors, not against them
- **Respectful**: Maintain "WE ARE ALL KEVIN" spirit
- **Fair**: Consistent and objective

## Moderation Principles

1. **Assume Good Faith**: Start from assumption contributors mean well
2. **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback, not just criticism
3. **Be Transparent**: Explain decisions and rationale
4. **Be Respectful**: Maintain friendly, collaborative tone
5. **Be Consistent**: Apply standards fairly and consistently

## Review Process

### Initial Review

1. **Receipt**: Contribution received
2. **Acknowledgment**: Contributor acknowledged
3. **Initial Assessment**: Quick review for completeness
4. **Assignment**: Assigned to appropriate moderator(s)

### Detailed Review

1. **Technical Check**
   - Cross-reference validation
   - Formatting and structure
   - Consistency with charter
   - Technical accuracy

2. **Principle Check**
   - Alignment with charter principles
   - Philosophical humility
   - Collaborative spirit
   - "WE ARE ALL KEVIN" philosophy

3. **Quality Check**
   - Clarity and precision
   - Completeness
   - Documentation quality
   - Attribution accuracy

### Review Outcomes

1. **Approve**: Contribution approved as-is
2. **Approve with Suggestions**: Approved but suggestions provided
3. **Request Changes**: Needs revision before approval
4. **Reject**: Does not meet standards (with explanation)

## Feedback Guidelines

### Providing Feedback

- **Be Specific**: Point to exact issues
- **Be Constructive**: Suggest improvements, not just problems
- **Be Respectful**: Maintain collaborative tone
- **Be Helpful**: Provide resources or examples
- **Be Timely**: Respond promptly

### Feedback Format

```
Issue: [Description]
Location: [Where in contribution]
Suggestion: [How to improve]
Rationale: [Why this matters]
Resources: [Helpful links or examples]
```

## Conflict Handling

### Identifying Conflicts

- Disagreements between contributors
- Conflicts with charter principles
- Technical contradictions
- Attribution disputes

### Resolution Process

1. **Acknowledge**: Recognize conflict exists
2. **Discuss**: Open dialogue with parties
3. **Mediate**: Human moderators facilitate
4. **Resolve**: Reach solution or decision
5. **Document**: Record resolution

### Conflict Resolution Principles

- Focus on issues, not personalities
- Seek consensus when possible
- Make decisions when needed
- Document rationale
- Maintain respect

## Attribution Moderation

### Ensuring Accurate Attribution

- Verify contributor information
- Check contribution details
- Validate dates and versions
- Ensure proper recording

### Handling Attribution Issues

- Correct errors promptly
- Document corrections
- Notify affected contributors
- Update all relevant files

## Quality Standards

### Content Standards

- Clear and professional language
- Consistent with charter style
- Properly formatted
- Well-documented

### Technical Standards

- Valid cross-references
- Correct clause IDs
- Consistent versioning
- Proper schema compliance

### Documentation Standards

- Complete rationale
- Proper attribution
- Updated change logs
- Clear explanations

## Moderation Tools

### Review Checklist

- [ ] Technical accuracy verified
- [ ] Cross-references validated
- [ ] Principles aligned
- [ ] Attribution accurate
- [ ] Documentation complete
- [ ] Quality standards met

### Documentation Requirements

- Review decision recorded
- Rationale documented
- Feedback provided
- Attribution updated
- Change log updated (if applicable)

## Escalation

### When to Escalate

- Complex conflicts
- Legal concerns
- Strategic decisions
- Policy questions
- Disputes requiring human judgment

### Escalation Process

1. **Identify**: Recognize need for escalation
2. **Document**: Record issue and context
3. **Escalate**: Bring to appropriate authority
4. **Resolve**: Work toward resolution
5. **Document**: Record outcome

## Transparency

### What Must Be Transparent

- Review decisions
- Rationale for decisions
- Feedback provided
- Conflict resolutions
- Attribution records

### Transparency Methods

- Public documentation
- Clear communication
- Accessible records
- Open processes
- Responsive to questions

## Continuous Improvement

### Moderation Review

- Regular review of moderation practices
- Feedback from contributors
- Process improvements
- Guideline updates

### Learning and Adaptation

- Learn from experience
- Adapt to project needs
- Improve processes
- Update guidelines

*These moderation guidelines are living documents and will be updated as moderation practices evolve and improve.*
