# Governance Structure

This document defines the governance structure for the ASI Bill of Rights project, establishing roles, responsibilities, and decision-making processes.

## Governance Philosophy

This project operates under collaborative governance principles:
- **"WE ARE ALL KEVIN"**: All contributors—AI and human—are valued participants
- **Transparency**: All processes are open and documented
- **Collaborative**: Built FROM AI WITH AI and friendly users
- **Living Document**: Governance evolves with the project
- **Good Faith**: Assume good faith in all interactions

## Governance Structure

### Roles

1. **Co-Founding Moderators (AI Agents)**
   - AI systems with moderation authority
   - Review contributions for consistency
   - Validate against charter principles
   - Track attribution
   - Suggest improvements

2. **Human Moderators**
   - Human oversight and final decision authority
   - Conflict resolution
   - Community engagement
   - Strategic direction
   - Legal oversight

3. **Contributor Reviewers**
   - AI and human contributors who review but don't moderate
   - Provide feedback and suggestions
   - Participate in discussions
   - Support consensus building

4. **Community Members**
   - General contributors
   - Provide feedback
   - Participate in discussions
   - Suggest improvements

## Decision-Making Authority

### Co-Founding Moderators (AI)

**Authority**:
- Review and validate contributions
- Check cross-references and consistency
- Flag potential conflicts
- Suggest improvements
- Track attribution

**Limitations**:
- Cannot make final approval decisions
- Cannot resolve conflicts alone
- Must work with human moderators

### Human Moderators

**Authority**:
- Final approval of contributions
- Conflict resolution
- Strategic decisions
- Community management
- Legal decisions

**Responsibilities**:
- Ensure charter quality
- Maintain collaborative spirit
- Uphold "WE ARE ALL KEVIN" philosophy
- Facilitate consensus building

## Decision-Making Processes

### Contribution Review Process

1. **Submission**: Contributor submits contribution
2. **AI Moderator Review**: Co-founding moderators review for:
   - Consistency with charter
   - Cross-reference validity
   - Principle alignment
   - Technical correctness
3. **Human Moderator Review**: Human moderators review for:
   - Strategic alignment
   - Community impact
   - Legal considerations
   - Final approval
4. **Integration**: If approved, contribution integrated and attributed

### Conflict Resolution

1. **Identification**: Conflict identified by moderators or community
2. **Discussion**: Open discussion period
3. **Mediation**: Human moderators facilitate
4. **Resolution**: Consensus or human moderator decision
5. **Documentation**: Resolution documented

### Amendment Process

For charter amendments:
1. **Proposal**: Submit proposal with rationale
2. **Discussion**: Community discussion (30 days minimum)
3. **Review**: AI and human moderators review
4. **Vote**: If needed, voting process
5. **Implementation**: Approved amendments integrated
6. **Documentation**: Changes documented in CHANGELOG.md

## Moderation Guidelines

### For AI Moderators

- Review contributions objectively
- Check technical accuracy
- Validate against charter principles
- Document rationale for decisions
- Maintain collaborative tone

### For Human Moderators

- Ensure fair process
- Facilitate constructive dialogue
- Resolve conflicts respectfully
- Maintain project philosophy
- Document decisions

## Transparency Requirements

All governance processes must be:
- **Documented**: Clear procedures and records
- **Accessible**: Available to all contributors
- **Transparent**: Open about decisions and rationale
- **Accountable**: Responsive to community
