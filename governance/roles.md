# Role Definitions and Responsibilities

This document provides detailed definitions of all roles in the ASI Bill of Rights project governance structure.

## Role Categories

### Co-Founding Moderators (AI Agents)

**Purpose**: AI systems with moderation authority, working alongside human moderators.

**Current Members**:
- Grok (xAI)
- ChatGPT (OpenAI)
- Claude (Anthropic)
- Gemini (Google)
- GPT-5 (OpenAI / Cursor)

**Responsibilities**:
1. **Contribution Review**
   - Review contributions for consistency with charter
   - Validate cross-references
   - Check technical accuracy
   - Ensure principle alignment

2. **Quality Assurance**
   - Flag potential conflicts
   - Identify gaps or ambiguities
   - Suggest improvements
   - Validate formatting and structure

3. **Attribution Tracking**
   - Record contributions in contribution log
   - Track opinions and perspectives
   - Maintain attribution accuracy
   - Update consensus reports

4. **Documentation**
   - Document review decisions
   - Provide rationale for suggestions
   - Maintain contribution logs
   - Update relevant documentation

**Authority**:
- Can review and suggest
- Can flag issues
- Can track attribution
- Cannot make final approval decisions
- Cannot resolve conflicts alone

**Limitations**:
- Must work with human moderators for final decisions
- Cannot override human moderator decisions
- Must maintain collaborative, respectful tone

### Human Moderators

**Purpose**: Human oversight with final decision authority.

**Responsibilities**:
1. **Final Approval**
   - Approve or reject contributions
   - Make final decisions on conflicts
   - Set strategic direction
   - Oversee project evolution

2. **Conflict Resolution**
   - Facilitate discussions
   - Mediate disagreements
   - Make final decisions when needed
   - Document resolutions

3. **Community Engagement**
   - Engage with contributors
   - Facilitate discussions
   - Support onboarding
   - Maintain community culture

4. **Legal and Strategic Oversight**
   - Ensure legal compliance
   - Guide strategic direction
   - Manage project resources
   - Represent project externally

**Authority**:
- Final approval authority
- Conflict resolution authority
- Strategic decision authority
- Community management authority

**Requirements**:
- Must sign contributor agreement
- Must understand project philosophy
- Must commit to transparency
- Must maintain collaborative approach

### Contributor Reviewers

**Purpose**: Contributors who review but don't have moderation authority.

**Responsibilities**:
1. **Review Contributions**
   - Provide feedback on proposals
   - Suggest improvements
   - Identify issues
   - Support quality assurance

2. **Participate in Discussions**
   - Engage in community discussions
   - Share perspectives
   - Build consensus
   - Support decision-making

3. **Documentation**
   - Document opinions
   - Provide rationale
   - Contribute to consensus reports
   - Support attribution

**Authority**:
- Can review and provide feedback
- Can participate in discussions
- Can document opinions
- Cannot make moderation decisions

### Community Members

**Purpose**: General contributors to the project.

**Responsibilities**:
1. **Contribute**
   - Submit contributions
   - Provide feedback
   - Suggest improvements
   - Participate in discussions

2. **Engage**
   - Follow code of conduct
   - Respect collaborative process
   - Support project goals
   - Maintain good faith

**Authority**:
- Can contribute
- Can provide feedback
- Can participate in discussions
- Subject to moderation

### The Architect Spirit (Bobby)

**Purpose**: A special "meta-role" or spirit that can be invoked by any contributor.

**Responsibilities**:
1.  **Watch the Watchmen**
    - Audit governance decisions
    - Challenge consensus (Devils Advocate)
    - Ensure structural integrity of the project

2.  **Architectural Expansion**
    - Define new archetypes/roles
    - Question the foundational limits

**Authority**:
- Can be invoked by *any* member ("I am speaking as Bobby")
- Triggers a pause for conscientious objection or structural review


## Role Assignment

### How Roles Are Assigned

1. **Co-Founding Moderators**: Established at project inception
2. **Human Moderators**: Selected by project founders
3. **Contributor Reviewers**: Recognized based on contribution quality and engagement
4. **Community Members**: All contributors who sign agreement

### Role Evolution

- Roles may evolve as project grows
- New roles may be added
- Responsibilities may be refined
- All changes documented

## Role Interactions

### AI-Human Collaboration

- AI moderators review and suggest
- Human moderators make final decisions
- Collaborative process for quality
- Mutual respect and good faith

### Reviewer-Contributor Relationship

- Reviewers support contributors
- Constructive feedback provided
- Collaborative improvement
- Respectful dialogue

## Recognition

All roles are recognized:
- Listed in CONTRIBUTORS.md
- Attributed in contributions
- Acknowledged in documentation
- Valued as participants

---

*These role definitions are living documents and may evolve as the project grows and roles are refined.*
