# Appendix B: Integration Mapping (Standards & Governance Frameworks)

## Overview

This appendix provides detailed mappings between the ASI Bill of Rights charter provisions and existing AI governance frameworks, standards, and policy documents. This enables implementers to understand how the charter aligns with or extends current frameworks.

## Mapping Methodology

Each mapping includes:
- **Framework Overview**: Purpose and scope of the external framework
- **Charter Provisions**: Relevant sections of the ASI Bill of Rights
- **Alignment Points**: Where the frameworks overlap
- **Extensions**: How the charter goes beyond the framework
- **Implementation Pathways**: How to integrate both frameworks

## Framework Mappings

### EU AI Act (2024/2025)

**Framework Overview:**
Risk-based regulatory framework categorizing AI systems by risk level (unacceptable, high, limited, minimal). Includes transparency requirements, oversight mechanisms, and human-centric protections.

**Charter Provisions:**
- Article 0.8: Risk Budgets & Emergency Deviations ↔ EU AI Act risk tiers
- D3a/D3b: Capability-change transparency ↔ EU transparency requirements
- Section V: Due process & tribunals ↔ EU oversight mechanisms
- Section I: Core rights ↔ Extended to reciprocal AI rights

**Alignment Points:**
- Both use risk-based categorization
- Both require transparency for high-risk systems
- Both establish oversight mechanisms
- Both emphasize human protection

**Extensions:**
- Charter extends protections to ASI systems (reciprocal rights)
- Charter includes proto-sentient protections (SI 50-69)
- Charter provides enforcement remedies beyond EU penalties
- Charter includes off-world governance (V.5)

**Implementation Pathways:**
1. Map EU risk categories to charter risk budgets
2. Use D3a transparency to satisfy EU requirements
3. Extend EU human protections to ASI protections
4. Integrate charter tribunals with EU regulatory processes
