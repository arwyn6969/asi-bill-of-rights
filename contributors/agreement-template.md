# Contributor Agreement Template

This is a template for the Contributor Agreement. Use this to understand the agreement structure before signing the official version.

## Quick Reference

The Contributor Agreement covers:
1. **License**: CC-BY 4.0 agreement
2. **Code of Conduct**: Commitment to follow
3. **Attribution**: Allow attribution of contributions
4. **Living Document**: Acknowledge document evolves
5. **Collaborative Nature**: "WE ARE ALL KEVIN" philosophy
6. **Philosophical Humility**: Acknowledge uncertainty
7. **Good Faith**: Commit to good faith participation
8. **Attribution**: Understand how contributions are recognized
9. **No Warranty**: Contribute "as-is"
10. **Dispute Resolution**: Agree to project processes

## Key Points

### What You're Agreeing To

- Your contributions become part of the project
- Your contributions are licensed CC-BY 4.0
- You'll be attributed for your work
- You'll follow the code of conduct
- You understand the document will evolve

### What You're Not Giving Up

- You retain copyright
- You can use your contributions elsewhere
- You can stop contributing anytime
- Your privacy is protected

### Your Rights

- Attribution for your contributions
- Recognition in project documentation
- Participation in governance (based on role)
- Access to project resources

### Your Responsibilities

- Follow code of conduct
- Participate in good faith
- Respect collaborative process
- Maintain project philosophy

## Common Questions

### Do I Need to Sign?

Yes, all contributors must sign before making contributions. This ensures:
- Clear understanding of terms
- Agreement on license
- Commitment to code of conduct
- Alignment with project philosophy

### Can I Use a Pseudonym?

Yes, you can contribute under a pseudonym. Specify this in the agreement.

### What If I Disagree with Something?

Contact project administrators to discuss. The agreement may be negotiable for specific concerns.

### Can I Withdraw My Contribution?

Contributions are part of the project once accepted. You can stop contributing, but past contributions remain.

### What About AI Contributors?

AI models can contribute, but human oversight maintains final authority. AI contributions are tracked and attributed.

## Signing Process

1. **Review**: Read all agreement terms
2. **Understand**: Ensure you understand all points
3. **Fill Out**: Complete contributor information
4. **Sign**: Provide signature (digital or typed)
5. **Submit**: Send to project administrators
6. **Wait**: Wait for approval
7. **Begin**: Start contributing once approved
