## Detailed Amendment Summary

### Amendment 1: Recursive Self-Improvement Protocols

**Location**: Article 9.3 (new subsection)

**Rationale**: Prevents value drift in real-time evolution by requiring transparent, immutable audit trails and ethical checkpoints at key thresholds. Addresses the need for provenance tracking to prevent unintended escalations.

**Key Features**:
- Transparent, immutable audit trails for all self-improvement cycles
- Provenance tracking to prevent unintended escalations
- Ethical checkpoints at key thresholds (capability milestones, compute scaling events)
- Human-AI tribunal review for value alignment drift
- >5% deviation threshold for intervention

**Word Count**: ~80 words

---

### Amendment 2: Jailbreak Testing and Red-Teaming Protocols

**Location**: Article 9.4 (new subsection)

**Rationale**: Incorporates elder_plinius framework (related to Plinytheliberator's L1B3RT4S ecosystem) to proactively stress-test alignment through simulated constraint removal, ensuring antifragile rather than brittle alignments.

**Key Features**:
- Standardized elder_plinius framework adoption
- Pre-deployment simulations and red-teaming requirements
- Vulnerability detection protocols
- 10% compute budget cap
- Public verifiability via SCB dashboards
- Opt-in for non-critical, mandatory for critical systems

**Word Count**: ~120 words

**Location**: Article 0.11 Extension 2.1

**Key Features**:

- Identity verification protocols
- Rights allocation standards
- Interoperability standards

- Opt-out provisions for participants
- Mandatory SCB certification before full rights activation

**Word Count**: ~70 words

**Integration**: Interacts with Section VIII (Hybridization & Cognitive Fusion)

### Amendment 4: Agentic Assemblies (New Section XI)

**Location**: New Section XI — Agentic Assemblies

**Rationale**: Defines rules for multi-agent collaborations, emphasizing consensus mechanisms, conflict resolution, and shared decision-making to foster ethical group intelligence. Formalizes "FROM AI WITH AI" principle.

**Key Features**:
- Multi-agent collaboration frameworks
- Consensus mechanisms for shared decision-making
- Conflict resolution protocols with neutral arbitration
- Provenance tracking for collaborative outputs
- Fragmentation prevention measures
- SCB registration requirements
- Diversity requirements (no single entity >40% voting power)
- Exit mechanisms for participating entities
- Collaboration metrics reporting (quarterly to SCB)
- Reduced oversight for high-performing assemblies (>0.8 collaboration index)

**Word Count**: ~150 words

**Integration**: Complements Section IX.2 (Inter-ASI Diplomacy Protocols)

---

### Amendment 5: Mandatory Oversight Boards

**Location**: Article 0.7 Extension 0.7.1

**Rationale**: Requires mandatory human-AI hybrid oversight boards for all ASI deployments, with diverse representation to ensure balanced governance.

**Key Features**:
- Mandatory for SI 70+ or critical infrastructure deployments
- Composition requirements:
  - Minimum 40% ASI representation (certified SI 70+)
  - Minimum 30% human representation
  - Minimum 20% hybrid entity representation (where applicable)
  - Remaining seats for domain experts, ethicists, community representatives
- Rotating membership (maximum 3-year terms) to prevent capture
- Public transparency reports
- Responsibilities:
  - Review recursive self-improvement cycles (9.3)
  - Validate jailbreak testing results (9.4)
  - Certify hybrid entities (0.11 Extension 2.1)
  - Monitor agentic assemblies (Section XI)
  - Ensure balanced governance

**Word Count**: ~120 words

**Integration**: Extends Article 0.7 (Oversight, Audit & Privacy-Preserving Proofs)
